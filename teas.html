black

darjeeling

DESCRIPTION

Darjeeling is a type of tea originating from the Darjeeling district in West Bengal, India. Although the tea is primarily classified as a black tea, white, green and oolong variations are also available.

FLAVOUR

thin / floral / musky

assam

DESCRIPTION

Assam black tea is a type of tea that originated from Assam, India. The tea plants are cultivated in the lowlands of Assam and yield significantly wider leaves than that of the Chinese variety.

FLAVOUR

bold / malty / brisk

earl grey

DESCRIPTION

Earl Grey black tea is a type of tea blend that combines black tea with the oil derived from Bergamot oranges. It was supposedly brought over to England from China as a gift for Charles Grey, the prime minister of the United Kingdom in the 1830s.

FLAVOUR

malty / sweet / bitter

masala chai

DESCRIPTION

Originating in the Indian subcontinent, Masala Chai, or “Mixed-Spiced Tea” in Hindi, is a type of tea blend made of black tea flavoured with various spices (the traditional base, or karha, is composed of ginger and cardamom), herbs, as well as milk.

FLAVOUR

warm / zesty / fragrant

oolong

tieguanyin

DESCRIPTION

Tieguanyin, or Iron Goddess of Mercy in Chinese, is a type of oolong tea that originated in the Anxi county of Fujian province, China. Its complex and highly sensitive production process involves plucking, withering, rolling, and oxidization.

FLAVOUR

light / fragrant / floral

ali shan

DESCRIPTION

Alishan, or Ali Mountain in Chinese, is a type of oolong tea originating from central Taiwan. The leaves are grown on Mount Ali at altitudes of 1000-1400 meters and are therefore hardier and more flavourful due to the high mountain conditions.

FLAVOUR

thin / floral / musky

green

sencha

DESCRIPTION

Sencha is the most popular type of Japanese green tea — accounting for approximately 80% of Japanese tea production. The production process includes heat processing the leaves through steaming, after which they are then rolled, shaped, and dried.

FLAVOUR

vegetal / grassy / oceanic (reminiscent of seaweed)

longjing

DESCRIPTION

Longjing, or "Dragon Well" in Chinese, is a type of pan-roasted green tea originating in Zhejiang province of China. It is supposedly named after how the sinuous motion of rain water overtop denser well water resembles that of a Chinese dragon.

FLAVOUR

smokey / grassy / nutty

ceylon

DESCRIPTION

Ceylon green tea is a tea originating from Sri Lanka (previously known as the British colony of Ceylon) that is lesser known compared to its black tea counterpart. Its production method includes either rolling shut or twisting open the tea leaves.

FLAVOUR

full / malty / nutty

jasmine

DESCRIPTION

Jasmine green tea is a type of green tea scented with the fragrance of jasmine flowers originating from the Ming dynasty of ancient China.

FLAVOUR

sweet / delicate / fragrant

white

bai mudan

DESCRIPTION

Bai Mudan, or “White Peony” in Chinese, is one of two major types of white tea originating from Fujian province, China. The tea is made from a combination of one leaf shoot (ie; leaf buds) and two immediate young leaves.

FLAVOUR

sweet / mellow / fruity

baihao yinzhen

DESCRIPTION

Baihao Yinzhen, or “White Hair Silver Needle” in Chinese, is one of two major types of white tea originating from Fujian province, China. The tea is named after the the downy white hairs retained by the shoots from which the tea is made.

FLAVOUR

delicate / sweet / fruity

herbal infusion.

herbal

chrysanthemum

DESCRIPTION

Dated back to the Song Dynasty of ancient China, chrysanthemum tea is an herbal infusion of dried chrysanthemum flowers that remains popular across East Asia today. Additional ingredients include rock or cane sugar, as well as goji berries.

FLAVOUR

delicate / sweet / floral

lemon ginger

DESCRIPTION

Historically, ginger infusion was used as an herbal medicine across Asia and the Middle East and have origins in several countries. Lemon ginger tea is made of ginger root, lemon juice, as well as a dash of honey.

FLAVOUR

sweet / bright / mildly spicy

sakurayu

DESCRIPTION

Sakurayu, or “Cherry Blossom Tea” in Japanese, is made from the infusion of pickled cherry blossom petals with hot water. The petals are harvested during mid-to-late spring, pickled in a solution of plum vinegar and salt, and dried.

FLAVOUR

salty / floral / sour

chamomile

DESCRIPTION

Chamomile tea is made from infusing dried Chamomile flowers with hot water. Although it is most often consumed as a recreational beverage, its contents include powerful antioxidants that serve significant benefits to human health.

FLAVOUR

earthy / floral / fruity (reminiscent of apple)

rooibos

DESCRIPTION

Rooibos tea is made from infusing the branches and twigs of the Aspalathus linearis tree with hot water and is the national drink of South Africa. It contains significant amounts of antioxidants, vitamin C, and polyphenols.

FLAVOUR

full / smokey / nutty
